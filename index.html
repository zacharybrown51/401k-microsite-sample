Plan Overview

The Mammini Company 401(k) Plan is designed to help you build a secure retirement future with generous employer contributions and flexible savings options.

Mammini Company contributes 3% of your eligible compensation to your 401(k) account - no employee contribution required! Plus, you may receive additional discretionary annual profit-sharing contributions. This is free money to help you save for retirement.

Plan Highlights

  • Eligibility: Age 21, immediate entry
  • Entry Date: Immediate upon eligibility
  • Employer Match: 3% non-elective safe harbor contribution
  • Profit Sharing: Discretionary annual contribution
  • Roth Option: Available
  • Loans: Allowed with standard provisions

Vesting Schedule

Your employer contributions vest according to the following schedule:

Service Period Vested %
Less than 1 year 0%
1 to 2 years 33%
2 to 3 years 66%
3+ years 100%

Your own contributions are always 100% vested.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about your 401(k) plan.

When can I start participating in the plan? ‚ñº
You can start participating immediately once you reach age 21. There's no waiting period - you can begin contributing and receiving employer contributions right away!
How much does Mammini Company contribute? ‚ñº
Mammini Company contributes 3% of your eligible compensation to your 401(k) account as a safe harbor non-elective contribution. Additionally, the company may provide discretionary annual profit-sharing contributions.
What is vesting and how does it work? ‚ñº
Vesting determines your ownership of employer contributions. Your own contributions are always 100% yours. Employer contributions vest as follows: 0% in your first year, 33% after 1 year, 66% after 2 years, and 100% after 3 years of service.
Can I take a loan from my 401(k)? ‚ñº
Yes, the plan allows loans subject to standard loan provisions. Contact Mammini Company or access your account portal for specific loan terms, limits, and application procedures.
What's the difference between Traditional and Roth contributions? ‚ñº
Traditional 401(k) contributions are made with pre-tax dollars (reducing your current taxable income), and you pay taxes when you withdraw in retirement. Roth 401(k) contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
How do I change my contribution amount or investments? ‚ñº
You can change your contribution amount or investment selections at any time by logging into your account at the Capital Group portal or by contacting Mammini Company directly.
What happens to my account if I leave the company? ‚ñº
If you leave Mammini Company, you keep your vested account balance. You can typically roll it over to an IRA or your new employer's plan, leave it in the plan (if balance requirements are met), or take a distribution (subject to taxes and potential penalties if under age 59¬Ω).
